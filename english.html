Interview with Brianna, a year 4 PhD student and TA

Brianna, a fourth-year anthropology PhD student at UCSD, discusses her experience teaching Spanish linguistics. In her second year of teaching, Brianna recognizes that while the course material focuses heavily on Latin American cultures, it lacks inclusion of East Asian cultures. She highlights how simple additions, such as including Asian countries when discussing topics like itineraries or prepositions in Spanish, could make the material more inclusive. She also suggests incorporating the cultural influences of countries like the Philippines, where Spanish is widely spoken, and acknowledging the Asian migration history in places like Chihuahua, Mexico.

Brianna proposes an easy change to improve inclusivity: asking students where they are from at the beginning of each quarter and recording that information. This would allow instructors to be more mindful and specific in drawing from students' diverse backgrounds, creating opportunities for cross-cultural discussions. By doing so, instructors could connect course material to studentsâ€™ lived experiences, making the learning environment feel more personal and inclusive for everyone, including East Asian students.

She emphasizes the importance of building a welcoming community in the classroom, especially in linguistics, where students are learning about languages to engage with different cultures. She praises the effort to make all students feel included and safe, noting that even small changes, like acknowledging cultural backgrounds, can make a significant difference. Brianna appreciates the opportunity to reflect on these issues and is committed to continuing the conversation with her colleagues to improve the classroom experience for all students.
